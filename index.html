    • Dutch immigrants

    In the 17th and 18th centuries, Dutch immigrants brought fried dough balls called olie koeken or olykoeks to New York. These dough balls were similar to modern donuts, but they didn't have the iconic ring shape.

    The word "doughnut"

    The word "doughnut" is generally agreed to have originated with writer Washington Irving's 1809 book A History of New York.

    The modern donut

    The modern donut likely originated in 19th century America. Donut franchises. In the 1940s and 1950s, donut franchises like Dunkin' Donuts and Krispy Kreme introduced mass-produced donuts. Doughnut flavors, shapes, and toppings Over the years, donuts have evolved to include a wide variety of flavors, shapes, and toppings.
